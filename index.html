Constrained optimization · KKT · trajectory optimization

Lagrangian Methods in Robotics

Interactive visualizations of Lagrange multipliers — the math under trajectory optimization, model predictive control, and the KKT conditions. At a constrained optimum, the constraint is tangent to a level set of the objective, so their gradients align:

∇f = λ ∇g
2D · contour

Tangency on the contour map

Drag a point around the constraint over the objective's contour rings. The ∇f and ∇g arrows rotate into alignment at the optimum, with a live readout of position, f, λ, and an alignment meter. Snap-to-optimum.
3D · surface

The constraint draped on the surface

The objective as a surface, the constraint a ribbon across it. The constrained optimum is the ribbon's high point; toggle a level curve to watch it go tangent at the crest. Orbit, zoom, snap-to-max/min, radius slider.
Source & full write-up on GitHub. No build step — vanilla HTML/CSS/JS; the 3D view uses Three.js from a CDN.
